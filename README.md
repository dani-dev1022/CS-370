## Connect your learning from throughout this course to the larger field of computer science:
### What do computer scientists do and why does it matter?
Computer scientists design and develop new and existing computing technology. Most obligations are to explore problems and solve, determine computing needs and system requirements, and analyze the results of their experiments. 

### How do I approach a problem as a computer scientist?
Before a solution can be proposed, the problem must be clearly identified. Once the problem is identified, it's important to document the solution before diving in to avoid wasting time by having to restart the process. The problem should be stated, as well as what should be functioning.  Additionally, discussing the issue and potential ideas with the product manager or system owners is crucial. Once the problem and solution are identified and confirmed, the solution can be implemented.

### What are my ethical responsibilities to the end user and the organization?
My integrity is essential in an organization where I am compliant, accountable, and reliable. I understand that no harm should come to the end users, and their data must be kept private and secure. This commitment to security also extends to the organization itself, ensuring that its data is protected.
